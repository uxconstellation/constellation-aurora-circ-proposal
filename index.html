Following our conversation with Lisa and the team, this proposal has been restructured to reflect Aurora's immediate priorities and budget. The focus is the main auroracirc.com site — fixing the foundation so the business can grow on solid ground.

01 — Context

The main site is leaving revenue on the table.

Aurora Circ is the only online marketplace in America for premium pre-owned luxury kitchens. The inventory is exceptional — Wolf, Sub-Zero, Poggenpohl, Eggersmann — but the site is not yet built to communicate that depth or convert the high-intent buyers who are actively searching for it.

Three core problems were identified on our call. This engagement addresses all three over six months.

Visibility gap

A hyper-theme migration resulted in thousands of 404 errors and broken redirects that are actively suppressing organic rankings for high-intent brand searches like Poggenpohl or Sub-Zero.

Conversion gap

New visitors are not funnelled effectively. The site doesn't communicate what Aurora is, what makes it different, or how to buy. Appliance inventory converts on eBay because that platform carries the trust signals the main site lacks.

Inventory depth gap

Aurora is a Wolf powerhouse. That is not communicated. The site reads as a flat e-commerce store rather than a luxury marketplace with genuine depth, curation, and brand authority.

No dedicated technical partner

Aurora currently has no ongoing technical relationship. Bug fixes, theme updates, and performance issues have no single point of accountability. This engagement changes that.

02 — Approach

Fix the foundation. Then scale.

Rather than building outward before the core is solid, this 6-month engagement is structured as a systematic improvement programme. Each month builds on the last. Milan's internal site knowledge is a key asset from day one.
